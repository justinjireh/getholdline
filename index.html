How it works

Faster than typing.
Simpler than a platform.

Holdline lives in your system tray and does exactly one thing, instantly.

01

Hold

Press and hold Ctrl + Space in any app — email, docs, chat, code, browser.

02

Speak

A thin line of light appears above your taskbar and rides your voice, so you always know it hears you.

03

Release

Your words are typed at the cursor — punctuated, capitalized, filler words removed if you like.

Privacy
“Nothing leaves your machine. Nothing is stored. Nothing to configure.”
●Processed on-device The speech model runs on your CPU. Dictation works with Wi-Fi off.
●No voice history Audio and transcripts are never written to disk. Holdline stores only your settings and custom words.
●No account, no telemetry There is nothing to sign into and nothing phoning home. Diagnostics, if you export them, never include audio or text.
Built lean, engineered hard

Every feature earns its place.

Instant capture

The microphone stays warm, so the first word is never clipped. Bluetooth headphones? Music-friendly mode releases the mic between dictations.

Custom words

Teach it names, brands, and jargon — capitalization preserved, stored locally, never uploaded.

Your hotkey

Ctrl+Space by default; change it to any combination. Release to type, Esc to cancel, re-paste the last dictation anytime.

Accuracy, your call

Automatic picks the best model for your hardware. Choose Fast for quick notes or Accurate for difficult speech.

Windows-native

A real tray utility — light and dark mode, high-DPI, reduced-motion support. Not a web app in a window.

Works everywhere

Types directly into whatever has focus, without touching your clipboard or clipboard history.

Questions

Fair questions, straight answers.

Does my voice really never leave my computer?

Really. The speech model is bundled inside the installer and runs locally. Dictation works with your network cable unplugged. Audio and transcripts are never saved — the only things Holdline writes to disk are your preferences and any custom words you add.

How is this different from Wispr Flow, Dragon, or Windows dictation?

Holdline is deliberately narrow: one hotkey, local English transcription, and direct typing into the active app. It does not require an account or a cloud speech service. The proposed launch price is $29 once.

How does the free trial work?

A 7-day Microsoft Store trial is planned for launch. Trial availability and regional terms will be confirmed on the Store listing before sales open.

Is there a Mac version?

It's in development — same idea, same privacy: hold a key, speak, release, all on-device. The beta is Windows-first; macOS follows. If you're on a Mac, join the beta list and say so, and you'll be first in line when it's ready.

What languages does it support?

Holdline v1 is tuned for English for the lowest latency. More languages are on the roadmap.

Do I need a powerful computer?

No. Holdline picks an appropriate local model and runs on ordinary Intel and AMD CPUs. The hardware beta will establish the final minimum and recommended specifications.

What about refunds?

Microsoft Store purchases follow the Store's return process and applicable law. Read the refund policy before buying.
